# Vision: FocusTimer

---

## Problem

Indie founders and solo builders lose 2–3 hours per day to context switching. They sit down to do deep work but end up in Slack, email, and browser tabs — not because they lack discipline, but because their tools don't protect their attention. Existing focus apps (Pomodoro timers, website blockers) treat symptoms without addressing the root cause: there's no shared contract between the founder and their work session about what "done" means before the session starts.

## Target user & JTBD

**Target user:** Solo founders and indie builders who work from home or coworking spaces, managing their own time without a manager or standup to create structure.

**Job to be done:** When I sit down to work on my most important project, I want to protect my next 90 minutes from interruption and drift, so I can ship something meaningful today without the guilt of lost hours.

## Vision statement

FocusTimer is the first focus tool built for solo founders: a session companion that turns vague work time into a committed, reviewable sprint — so at the end of the day, you know exactly what you shipped and why it mattered.

## Why now

Remote and async work has decoupled output from presence. Solo founders no longer have office structure to create accountability — but the tools available are still built for co-located teams (shared calendars, standups, status updates). The Pomodoro-app market has not evolved since 2010 despite a fundamental shift in how independent builders work.

## 3-year picture

In three years, FocusTimer is the default session tool for 50,000 indie founders. Each founder using FocusTimer ships one more meaningful project per quarter than they did before. The average user has a logged record of 500+ sessions showing what they built, when, and what changed — a personal operating history.

## Non-goals

- **Not a team coordination tool.** No shared workspaces, no team dashboards, no manager views.
- **Not a habit tracker.** We will not gamify streaks or send motivational push notifications.
- **Not a website blocker.** Distraction blocking is intentionally out of scope in v1.
- **Not a task manager.** The session is the unit, not the task list.

## North Star metric

**Sessions completed with a pre-defined intention** per active user per week.

A session counts when: (1) the user defines what they're working on before starting, and (2) the session runs to completion without abandonment.

## Key risks

- **Value risk:** Solo founders have tried many focus tools and abandoned them. Why will they form a lasting habit with FocusTimer? The pre-session intention ritual is the bet — we need to validate this increases retention before scaling.
- **Usability risk:** The intention-setting step must feel lightweight, not like homework. If it takes more than 60 seconds to define an intention, founders will skip it.
- **Feasibility:** Low. The core product is a timer + a structured text input. No novel technical risk in v1.
