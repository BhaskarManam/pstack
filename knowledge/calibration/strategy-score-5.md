# Product Strategy: FocusTimer v1

**Based on:** `00-vision.md` (score: 4.8)
**Horizon:** next 2 quarters | **Status:** Committed bet

---

## The bet

We are betting that **the intention-setting ritual — not the timer — is FocusTimer's wedge.** For the next two quarters we do one thing: make stating and honoring an intention the most frictionless, rewarding loop in the category. Everything is in service of *intentional sessions completed per active user per week*.

**We are explicitly NOT doing this period, and why:**
- **No team/collaboration features** — our wedge is the solo deep-work loop; multiplayer dilutes the ritual and pulls us into a crowded category. Revisit only after the solo loop retains.
- **No calendar / task-manager integrations** — integrations make us a feature of someone else's product before we've proven we're a product. They also import other apps' framing of "work," which fights our intention framing.
- **No AI intention suggestions** — we must learn what users write unaided before we optimize it; suggesting now corrupts the signal we're trying to read.

## Segment & sequencing

**First segment:** solo founders and indie makers who already block focus time but abandon sessions mid-way — not students, not corporate knowledge workers. They have the pain acutely, they self-fund tools, and they talk publicly (Twitter/X, IndieHackers), giving us a cheap distribution loop.

**Sequence (why this order de-risks the vision fastest):**
1. **Q1 — Prove the ritual retains** for solo founders on iOS only. One platform, one segment: fastest path to a clean retention read.
2. **Q2 — Widen the ritual's reward loop** (streaks, honest "meaningful vs. partial" history) for the *same* segment before adding any new segment. We expand depth before breadth because the bet is about the loop, not the audience.

We deliberately do not pursue Android or a second segment until the solo-founder retention curve flattens above target — adding surface area before the loop works would blur the retention signal.

## The insight

From 14 exit interviews and two prototypes: **users didn't quit sessions because the timer was wrong — they quit because they forgot why they started.** Prototype B (verbal intention prompt) lifted completion from 34% → 61% and 5-day retention from 22% → 41%, and 9 of 14 users named "I remembered what I said I'd do" as the reason they didn't stop early.

The non-obvious part: the entire category optimizes the timer (durations, sounds, blocking). The retention lever isn't the timer at all — it's a 10-second self-accountability contract the incumbents treat as a throwaway text field. We can win a category we don't out-time, by out-*meaning* it.

## Success definition

| Metric | Role | Baseline | Target | Window |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Intentional sessions / active user / week | **North Star** (value) | — (new) | ≥ 3.0 | Day 60 |
| Session completion rate | Input (leading) | 34% | ≥ 58% | Day 30 |
| % sessions with intention typed | Input (activation quality) | — (new) | ≥ 60% | Week 2 |
| 14-day retention | Input (health) | 22% | ≥ 38% | Day 14 |
| Median time intention-screen → start | **Guardrail** | — | ≤ 45s | ongoing |

**Winning looks like:** completion ≥ 58% AND the intention-typers vs. skippers retention gap is > 20pp (the ritual is the mechanism).
**Losing looks like:** completion < 50% at day 30 OR the typer/skipper gap < 10pp (the ritual is not the driver) — at which point we treat it as a *product* failure and redesign the ritual, not the timer.

## Trade-offs (what this bet costs us)

- **We under-serve teams and power-users for two quarters** — the largest willingness-to-pay segment. If a competitor lands a great solo ritual *and* team features, we lose the high end. We accept this because spreading thin loses the wedge first.
- **iOS-only delays half the addressable market** and risks an Android-first competitor. Accepted: a clean retention read is worth more right now than reach.
- **No integrations means lower day-1 stickiness** (we don't live where users already are). Accepted: integrating before the loop works would make us a forgettable feature, not a habit.

**What would prove this bet wrong:** if the ritual retains no better than a plain timer for our own segment (typer/skipper gap < 10pp), the entire thesis — that meaning beats timing — is false, and the vision needs rethinking, not just the roadmap.
